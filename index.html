PolitiColor
PolitiColor maps Twitter sentiment data in real-time for the 2016 Presidential Election

What is Politicolor?

PolitiColor is an app that converts tweets into sentiment information on the 2016 Presidential Election. Using a chroropleth map, Twitter opinions expressed in tweets are color coded and mapped by state. Users can quickly view sentiment data on their favorite candidates, or on general sentiment between the two major parties. When Twitter opinion in a state swings in favor of a candidate, their color appears more prominantly; if the opposite occurs, the opposing color is displayed.

PolitiColor is just in time for election season. With campaigns and the country gearing up for the first caucuses and primaries, Politicolor provides a unique outlet for information that is difficult to gather and arguably more accurate than the tried and true method of polls. Politicolor is real time and digital; it can be accessed everywhere and is constantly updating. As a result, even the most tireless of political junkies can get their fill of data and analytics.

How PolitiColor works

The Hose

Twitter is in many ways the heartbeat of the population. Where most people see social media, Politicolor sees data. By tracking thousands of tweets per hour, Politicolor can one-up polls and get a more accurate representation of how people actually feel.

The Engine

The Engine is the centerpiece of Politicolor. To function, Politicolor needs to convert the barely-english of Twitter into something readable for machines. The Engine, composed of a unique machine learning POS algorithm, does just that.

The Calculator

Each individual tweet is a small drop in a huge ocean; to see real data connections, Politicolor uses The Calculator to aggregate the data and draw out meaningful information.

The Map

There is a lot of information being distilled and displayed very rapidly, so design and polish must be the watchwords of Politicolor. The Map above, built to be easily understood, works to draw the user in.
